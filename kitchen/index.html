From the Kitchen

Modern American chef with desert bones.

2025 FavChef Semifinalist

The Background

I paid for college working as a chef. Line cook, prep cook, eventually running kitchens. The discipline transferred: build systems that don't depend on heroics. Document everything. Remove what doesn't earn its place.

In 2025, I was selected as a semifinalist in FavChef, a home cooking competition. Three signature dishes, months of development, and the kind of obsessive refinement that only makes sense if you love the work.

The Philosophy

I cook from the Sonoran desert tradition I grew up with, filtered through years of professional technique. Chiles, citrus, smoke. Clean flavors, honest ingredients, nothing on the plate that doesn't belong there.

Same principle applies to my professional work. Clarity over complexity. Systems that scale. Nothing that's just there to look impressive.

Signature Dishes

Spring's First Breath

Plating & Presentation

Charred leek ash gnocchi, sweet pea purée, black garlic gel, micro radish, lemon air. A whisper of spring. Vibrant, airy, and soulful. A celebration of renewal and restraint.

Mi Tierra

Cultural Infusion

Duck leg confit, Mole Colorado, blue corn grit cake, pickled red onion, pepita dust. A soulful bridge between Mexican tradition and the American Southwest. Earthy, rich, and unapologetically honest.

Fire & Foie

Innovative Combos

Foie gras ice cream, warm financier, burnt honey gastrique, charred grapes, crispy chicken skin. A study in contrast. Heat meets chill, sweet meets savor, and risk meets reward.

Kitchen Principles

Mise en place is mindset

Everything in its place before the fire is lit. Preparation isn't just practical. It's how you approach the work.

Technique over tricks

A properly seared protein doesn't need foam or spherification. Master the fundamentals before adding complexity.

Restraint is a choice

The hardest decision is what to leave off the plate. Every element should earn its place. If it's just decoration, cut it.

Document everything

Recipes are systems. Write them down well enough that someone else could execute them. That's how you improve.

Built by hand. No templates.
