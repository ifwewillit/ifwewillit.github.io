How I Cook

I'm a story-driven, place-based chef. I don't cook to impress with tricks. I cook to say something true about where I've been and who I am now. Every dish has to earn its place. If it doesn't say something, it doesn't belong on the plate.

I lead with savory flavors. Depth matters more than sweetness. I use heat on purpose: smoke, char, bitterness, and fat are tools, not effects. My food is minimalist but not delicate. Clean plates, strong flavors. I remove more than I add.

Modern American chef with desert bones.

Influences

  • American Southwest — chiles, smoke, restraint
  • New Zealand — honesty, simplicity, letting protein speak
  • Pine Ridge — respect for scarcity, resilience
  • Colorado — contrast, altitude, clean finishes

Flavor Biases

Umami, bitterness, smoke, acidity. Food that has weight and tension. Little patience for trend chasing or sauces that exist just to exist.

The kitchen taught me systems thinking. A good kitchen doesn't rely on cooks remembering orders. Ticket comes in, stays on the rail until food goes out. The system makes status obvious. I run organizations the same way.
